About

Welcome to ComboJunkie, a personal blog dedicated to rhythm games and the experiences that come with playing them. This website was created out of a genuine interest in rhythm games and the unique feeling they give when gameplay and music perfectly sync together.

I've always found rhythm games interesting because they are different from most other types of games. Instead of focusing only on story or combat, rhythm games challenge your timing, focus, and reaction while letting you enjoy music at the same time. There is something really satisfying about hitting notes perfectly, maintaining a long combo, and seeing your score improve after multiple attempts.

ComboJunkie started as a simple idea: a place where I could write about the rhythm games I play and share my thoughts with other people who enjoy the same genre. Rather than just playing a game and moving on, I wanted to take time to reflect on the experience, what makes the gameplay fun, how the music affects the difficulty, and whether the game is enjoyable for both casual and experienced players.

On this blog, you will mostly find reviews and impressions of rhythm games from different platforms. Some of the titles featured here include Muse Dash, osu!, Phigros, Cytus II, MUSYNX, and KALPA: Cosmic Symphony. Each review is based on my personal experience playing the game — what I liked, what stood out to me, and what kind of players might enjoy it.

This blog isn't meant to be overly technical or overly critical. Instead, it's more like sharing thoughts and experiences with other rhythm game fans. Whether you are someone who casually plays rhythm games for fun or someone who enjoys challenging charts and chasing high scores, the goal of ComboJunkie is to create a space where rhythm game enthusiasts can explore and appreciate the genre.

At the end of the day, ComboJunkie is simply a personal project fueled by my interest in rhythm games. It's a place where I can talk about the games I enjoy and hopefully introduce others to rhythm games they might not have tried before.

If you're someone who enjoys rhythm games, discovering new music through gameplay, or pushing yourself to get that perfect combo, then you'll probably feel right at home here.
